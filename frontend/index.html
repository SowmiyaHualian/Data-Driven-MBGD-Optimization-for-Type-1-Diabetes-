Frequently Asked Questions

What is Type-1 Diabetes? ▼
Type-1 Diabetes is an autoimmune condition where the pancreas doesn't produce enough insulin. It typically develops in children and young adults, though it can occur at any age. Unlike Type-2 Diabetes, it's not preventable through lifestyle changes alone.
How accurate is DiagnoSync? ▼
DiagnoSync uses a machine learning model trained on clinical data from the ICMR repository. While highly accurate, this tool is for informational purposes only and should not replace professional medical diagnosis. Always consult a healthcare provider for official diagnosis.
What data do I need to provide? ▼
You'll need to provide basic health metrics including age, BMI, fasting glucose levels, random glucose levels, HbA1c percentage, and information about any symptoms you may be experiencing (polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss, fatigue, blurred vision).
Is my data safe? ▼
Yes, your data is stored securely in an encrypted database. We use industry-standard security practices including password hashing with bcrypt and JWT token-based authentication. Your data is never shared with third parties.
What should I do if I get a high-risk result? ▼
If you receive a high-risk assessment, we recommend scheduling an appointment with an endocrinologist or your primary care physician. They can perform official diagnostic tests including fasting glucose tests, glucose tolerance tests, and HbA1c measurements.
Can children use DiagnoSync? ▼
DiagnoSync can be used for individuals of all ages, but for children and minors, we strongly recommend parental supervision and consultation with a pediatrician. Children showing symptoms should receive immediate medical attention.
How often should I get tested? ▼
This depends on your risk level and family history. If you have a family history of diabetes or received a moderate/high risk result, annual screening is recommended. Consult your healthcare provider for a personalized testing schedule.
